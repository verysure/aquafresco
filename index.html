About AquaFresco

Water scarcity has become one of the greatest challenges of our generation, impacting billions of people. Our solution to the problem is to improve the efficiency of water usage, in particular, for laundry and dish washing which accounts for more than 20% of domestic water consumption. The current cleaning process utilizes significant amount of water and detergent to remove less than 1% of hydrophobic "garbage" (grease or stain). We hypothesize that a better solution is to develop a filter which selectively takes out the "oil" and recycle the water and detergent for future cleaning. The invention won the first place of the MIT MADMAC 10K competition this year.

To achieve this goal, we developed a polymer foam filter, AquaFresco, which is engineered to selectively absorb hydrophobic compounds while rejecting water and detergent which can be subsequently recycled to the laundry machine. Prototype studies show that when incorporated into a household laundry machine just one batch of water and detergent can be used to wash clothes up to 7 months. We will no longer require additional water and detergent for laundry. This powerful greywater regeneration device can save thousands of liters of water per month (>20% of in-door water consumption) and kilograms of detergent which would otherwise be in the drain, polluting the environment. Potentially, this oil selective filter can also be implemented in other greywater system, for example, treating the oil-rich wastewater from the hydraulic fracturing process.
